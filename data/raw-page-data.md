## About AIC Mahindra
AIC Mahindra e-Hub Foundation is a Section 8 not-for-profit company and an Atal Incubation Centre supported by the Atal Innovation Mission, NITI Aayog, Government of India. Established by Mahindra University to support early-stage startups, entrepreneurs and innovators in building products, validating business models and creating ventures. Works with startups at early/critical stages: problem validation, prototyping, product development, user testing, market access, funding preparedness and business growth. Access to mentors, prototyping infrastructure, lab facilities, business guidance, industry experts, investor connects and ecosystem partners.

## Startup Pathways and Founder Support
Intro: AIC Mahindra supports founders at different stages of their startup journey, from early ideas to validated products and incubation-ready ventures.

Tabs:
1. Eureka Evenings — monthly idea pitch platform for MU students (UG/PG/PhD); informal space to pitch to the AIC team, get feedback, explore next steps; encourages students and guides serious teams towards pre-incubation.
2. IdeaSprint — three-day, in-person idea validation bootcamp for early-stage founders/student teams; problem validation, customer discovery, assumption testing, peer feedback.
3. Startup Clinic — focused mentoring; book a 30-minute session (online or in person) on one or two key challenges (problem validation, customer discovery, product direction, business model, go-to-market, pitch prep, funding readiness, early operations).
4. Pre-Incubation — hybrid, four-month engagement; intensive on-campus bootcamp + virtual mentoring; customer discovery, idea validation, prototype planning, business model development, mentor reviews, pitch prep; concludes with a final pitch day.
5. Lab-to-Market Program — supports faculty, researchers and PhD scholars with research-led innovations; explores use cases, customer segments, market relevance, prototype development, startup pathways; moves technology beyond the lab to pilots/products/partnerships/venture creation.
6. Incubation — rolling calls for applications; selection via application review, evaluation and final pitch to the incubation panel; up to 18 months of support (mentoring, workspace, prototyping/lab access, business guidance, market connects, funding readiness, investor linkages, ecosystem support).
